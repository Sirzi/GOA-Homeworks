Biography

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien (1892–1973) was an English writer, philologist, and academic. He is best known for his high-fantasy works, including "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings." Tolkien's impact on literature, particularly fantasy literature, is unparalleled. His works have been translated into many languages and have inspired numerous adaptations in film, television, and more.

Major Works

  • The Hobbit - 1937
  • The Lord of the Rings - 1954–1955
  • The Silmarillion - Posthumously published in 1977
  • The Children of Húrin - 2007
  • Unfinished Tales - 1980
