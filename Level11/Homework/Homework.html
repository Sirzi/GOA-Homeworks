Elephant

The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. Known for their intelligence and strong familial bonds, they are found primarily in Africa and Asia.

Tiger

The tiger is the largest cat species and a fierce predator. It is known for its orange coat and black stripes, with a diet that includes deer and wild boar.

Giraffe

The giraffe is the tallest animal on land, reaching heights up to 18 feet. Their long necks help them to reach leaves at the tops of trees in the African savannah.

Back to top

Lion

The lion is known as the "king of the jungle." Males have a distinctive mane, and lions live in social groups called prides.

Whale

Whales are the largest marine animals. The blue whale is the largest animal ever known, even bigger than any dinosaur.

Rhinoceros

The rhinoceros is known for its thick skin and horn. Rhinos are often found in Africa and Asia, where they graze on grass and bushes.

Polar Bear

The polar bear is the largest land carnivore and is native to the Arctic Circle. They are excellent swimmers and rely on sea ice to hunt seals.

Hippopotamus

The hippopotamus, or "hippo", is a large, mostly herbivorous mammal found in rivers and lakes across Africa. Despite their size, they can run fast on land.

Back to top

Gorilla

The gorilla is the largest primate and shares over 98% of its DNA with humans. They live in groups led by a dominant male known as a silverback.

Back to top

Kangaroo

The kangaroo is a marsupial native to Australia. Known for their powerful hind legs, kangaroos can cover large distances in a single bound.
